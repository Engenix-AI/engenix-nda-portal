Decision anatomy

Every task should answer the questions the operator already has.

A guided operating view is not a generic task list. It is intended to provide the decision context that lets an authorized user act with appropriate speed and accountability.

01

What happened

A concise description of the operating event.

02

Why it matters

The potential customer, financial, compliance or workflow consequence.

03

Recommended next move

A management action for qualified dealership review—not an autonomous decision.

04

Owner and deadline

The person responsible and the point at which management attention is required.

The abstracted source records relevant to dealership review.

Role-based views

The same transaction. A different responsibility.

Guided Experience is designed to organize the relevant work around the person accountable for the next move, while retaining access to the broader intelligence workspace.

Dealer Principal

Material exposure, unresolved leadership decisions and rooftop-level operating patterns.

General Manager

Deals requiring intervention, accountable owners and operating deadlines.

Desk Manager

Structure decisions and unresolved approval context.

F&I Manager

Ownership decisions, review requirements and funding readiness.

Compliance Leader

Findings, evidence, corrective action and decision history.

Controller

Reconciliation, financial exposure and close readiness.

Human-controlled

Guidance when needed. Depth when required.

Guided Experience does not replace dealership policy, employee training, legal review or human responsibility. It is being prepared to organize the issue and supporting evidence; qualified dealership personnel remain responsible for the action taken.
